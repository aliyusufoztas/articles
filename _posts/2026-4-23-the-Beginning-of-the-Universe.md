When we look around, we see an incredible variety of objects; from the trees and animals to the sky and sun. But what existed when there were no trees, no animals, no sky, no sun, and even no time? What existed before everything in our universe? We know our universe has a beginning, and in this article, we are going to explore some theories about the beginning of our universe.

Historically, there were many different ideas about the origin of our universe. Many scientists believed the universe had no beginning. This theory was called the 'Steady State' theory. According to this theory, our universe had always existed. However, new research and recent discoveries changed scientists' ideas in the last century. Today, strong evidence shows us that our universe has a beginning. Ultimately, the most widely accepted theory today is the 'Big Bang Theory', which tells us our universe began from a single point called 'singularity'.

## The Big Bang Theory: How Our Universe Began from a Single Point?
When anyone asks us, "How did our universe begin?", we can simply answer: "With an explosion". Yes, the Big Bang was an explosion, but it was not a typical one like TNT or dynamite. A simple TNT or dynamite explosion just throws and damages the objects; but the Big Bang was not really like that, it marked the beginning of our universe. It was not just a explosion, it was also an expansion.

### The Explosion of the Singularity and the Expansion of the Cosmos
The Big Bang theory explains that the explosion which started the beginning phase of the universe brought the rapid expansion of the cosmos itself. It began from an infinitely small single point called 'singularity', where the all matter and energy were concentrated. It contained the entire universe and also time itself, yet it had no size. With the explosion of this point, our universe has began.

### The Cosmic Inflation: A Result of the Rapid Expansion
Following the very beginning of our universe, a process called 'Cosmic Inflation' occurred. In a fraction of a second-much faster than even the speed of light, the universe expanded exponentially. The no-sized 'singularity' point became the size of a galaxy in almost no time (10^{-32} \text{ seconds})$). This rapid exponential growth smoothed out the universe and prevented it from collapsing back into itself due to gravity. As the result of this very short phase, today we can see the sun, the moon, and anything else exists in our universe.
