When we look around, we see an incredible variety of objects; from the trees and animals to the sky and sun. But what existed when there were no trees, no animals, no sky, no sun, and even no time? What existed before everything in our universe? We know our universe has a beginning, and in this article, we are going to explore some theories about the beginning of our universe.

Historically, there were many different ideas about the origin of our universe. Many scientists believed the universe had no beginning. This theory was called the 'Steady State' theory. According to this theory, our universe had always existed. However, new research and recent discoveries changed scientists' ideas in the last century. Today, strong evidence shows us that our universe has a beginning. Ultimately, the most widely accepted theory today is the 'Big Bang Theory', which tells us our universe began from a single point called 'singularity'.

## The Big Bang Theory: How Our Universe Began from a Single Point?
When anyone ask us 'How our universe began', we can simply answer it like 'With an explosion.' Yes, the 'Big Bang' is simply an explosion, but that's not a typical explision like an explosion of a TNT or a dynamite. With explosion of a TNT or dynamite, cannot begin a whole universe: but with the 'Big Bang' explosion, began our universe.

The 'Big Bang' idea tells us that it was a explosion that bringed the rapid expansion of space. It began with a single point called 'singularity', where the all matter and energy were concentrated in an infinitely small point. Following the big explosion of this small point and huge expansion of our universe, the 'Cosmic Inflation' occured and this caused our universe grow exponentially.
